01 / research idea

Systems are not only dynamic in state. They are dynamic in size.

Many models hold the number of entities fixed. But real systems constantly change their population: neurons strengthen, weaken, appear, or die; animals enter and leave habitats; vehicles appear and disappear from traffic; buildings emerge and decay in cities.

birth

New entities

How does structure change when new agents, cells, vehicles, or buildings appear?

death

Entity loss

What remains stable when parts of the system disappear or stop participating?

scaling

Changing size

Which patterns survive when a system grows from a few entities to many?

rewiring

Changing interactions

How do local relations reorganize when the population itself is moving?
